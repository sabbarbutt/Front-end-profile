Kinglet

Think

Kinglet caters to work place real estate. This stood as a challenge for me since my design capacity had not expanded into this domain. I began by researching the user experience of a multitude of real estate websites. I try to understand the user interface of real estate website and how I could adapt this general theory on to kinglet necessities.

Design

Once the thought process was complete, I began creating a prototype wireframes and design idea for the entire application. I put them for review and with a little back and forth between me and client we were able to finalize the existing design .We both felt that this design answer user interface and user experience issue.

Build

The Site was made responsive with bootstrap, Using LESS & SASS I was able to create an environment that was soft on the eye no matter what device you visited the site on. In order to ensure adaptability across platforms I tested the site on all devices to make sure there were no bugs in the display.

Fully Responsive Website

With a rise in the use of handheld devices, a great many number of tasks take place not on our desktops but rather on our tabs and mobiles. Kinglet was keen to capture the ever growing market and to do so we had to make their website 100% responsive.

Better search listing

Nothing tells a better story than a picture, and in order to give users the freedom to see work places and choose based on pictures we created a better means through which kinglet was able to display search listings. Images with easy piece of property really paints the picture for potential clients

Custom Map Navigator

Sticking with this theme of pictures put together in the listings page, I was able to create another functionality which further helped enhance user experience. I was able to create a custom map search which not only pinpointed the real estate location, but also provided images for the property as a sidebar.
